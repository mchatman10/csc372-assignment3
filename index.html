Are you located near the UNCG Petty Building?

Here are some restaurants that you should try!

This blog will provide 3 unique recommendations for food establishments that are nearby UNCG. For each restaurant, there are 3 personally recommended dishes to choose from. Each one will surely leave you salivating! The best part? No car necessary! Each establishment is within a half-mile radius of the Petty building!

Don, Japanese Restaurant - Ishiyaki, Donburi & Noodle

Don, Japanese Restaurant - Ishiyaki, Donburi & Noodle, located at 423 Tate St, has an array of Japanese cuisine, including rice bowls, noodles, salads, and deserts. They're open from 11AM-3:30PM, and 5-10PM Monday through Friday, and 11:30AM-10PM on Saturdays.
Steamed Rice Bowl DishPork cutlet, egg and onions simmered in dashi sauce served over rice. Chicken YakisobaYou can also pick between other various types of ramen and broths. Green Tea Ice CreamOne of their three different ice cream choices.

Romeo's Vegan Burgers

Romeo's Vegan Burgers, located at 413 Tate Street, boasts a 100% plant-based menu, featuring popular options like the "Where Art Thou Romeo Burger," "Romeo Mac Burger," and the "Tobacco Road Burger". You can choose from Impossible, Beyond, or house-made black bean patties. They're open from 11AM-10PM.
Romeo's Chili DogThe vegan chili, which is also used on their chili cheese fries and tots, is made with a combination of vegan ground beef, beans, and other ingredients. Romeo Mac BurgerThe Romeo Mac Burger is made with a plant-based patty, topped with mac sauce, onions, and house pickles. Crispy Chicken Kale SaladServed with Romeo's house-made ranch dressing.

Raaz Indian Kitchen

Raaz Indian Kitchen is known for combining traditional Indian cuisine with modern twists and offers a wide variety of menu options, including curries, biryanis, and tandoori specialties. They're open Monday through Saturday from 11:30AM-10PM.
Tikka MasalaChoice of meat marinated in yogurt sauce then grilled in a clay oven before the meat is dipped in a rich silky gravy and simmered for consistency. Dal TadkaLentil stew simmered in simple spices and tempered. Garlic NaanFlour bread baked in clay oven.
Deciding between 3 different restaurants can be very tough! My personal pick out of the 3? Easily Raaz Indian Kitchen. I'm a sucker for some good Indian cuisine, and the tikka misala hits the spot, every time. Another dish from Raaz that went unmentioned in this blog is the butter chicken. I highly recommend it, and it goes especially well with the garlic naan.
